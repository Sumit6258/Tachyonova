What is
Tachyonova?

Tachyonova is a theoretical research project studying what a distributed ledger would look like if its architecture obeyed superluminal physics. It draws from the tachyon — a hypothetical particle whose rest mass is imaginary, which forces it to always travel faster than c. As it loses energy, it accelerates. The network mirrors this behaviour.

We are not building a faster blockchain. We are asking what a blockchain would be if the transfer of value was governed not by computation time, but by the geometry of spacetime itself.

The Light Speed Barrier

In general relativity, as any massive object accelerates toward c, its relativistic momentum diverges to infinity. The barrier is not a wall — it is an asymptote. No conventional network can cross it. Tachyons, however, do not approach c from below. They exist only above it. This is the regime Tachyonova studies.

The Tachyon
Core

At the centre of the Tachyonova model sits a conceptual engine — the Tachyon Core. It is not hardware. It is a theoretical construct: a region of the protocol where state transitions are treated as quantum field events rather than sequential computations. Information does not travel through it. It propagates across it, instantaneously, the way a phase transition moves through a superfluid.

The Core is a model for zero-latency consensus — not achieved through speed, but through a reimagining of what it means for state to exist in a distributed system.
